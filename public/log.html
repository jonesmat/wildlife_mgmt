💡 Tip: Record the count and a sex/age breakdown ("2 bucks, 3 does, 1 fawn") — consistent sightings become incidental census data. Set the map location and attach game-camera photos when you have them; they're strong supporting documentation.
💡 Tip: Pick the category matching one of the seven 1-D-1 qualifying practices — "Update from Activity Log" on the annual report checks the Part II box for you. Record acres treated and take before/after photos; appraisal districts want evidence the practice actually happened.
💡 Tip: Run the same route under the same conditions each time — TPWD recommends at least three spotlight counts on a fixed route in late summer/fall. Record route, conditions, and totals consistently so year-over-year trends are comparable.

💡 Tip: Log every removal with species, method, and count — these document the predator-control qualifying practice on your annual report. Brown-headed cowbird trapping requires a free TPWD trapping certification; note trap locations on the map.
💡 Tip: Upkeep of wildlife infrastructure — feeders, water troughs, nest boxes, fences — counts as ongoing practice implementation. Name the specific item and what was done; a quick photo of the completed work makes good documentation.
